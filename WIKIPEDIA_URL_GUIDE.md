## ğŸ¯ Best Practices

1. **Use Specific Articles:** Always use the most specific article URL
2. **Check for Disambiguation:** If you see a list of topics, you're on a disambiguation page
3. **Use Parentheses:** Add clarifying parentheses for ambiguous terms
4. **Test the URL:** Open the URL in a browser first to verify it's a full article
5. **Look for Content:** Make sure the article has substantial text content (not just lists)

### "This is a disambiguation page"
- **Meaning:** The URL points to a page that lists multiple topics
- **Solution:** Use a specific article URL with parentheses

### "Failed to extract sufficient content"
- **Meaning:** The page has very little text content
- **Solution:** Try a different, more detailed article

- **Meaning:** The URL doesn't match Wikipedia's format
- **Solution:** Use format: `https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Article_Name`
